PROGRESSION SYSTEM

The 4th Prussian Regiment was ordered to be formed on 13 November 1806 by means of a letter from Marshal Berthier on behalf of Napoleon I to Prince Isenburg. Napoleon instructed Isenburg to select officers for the new foreign regiment from Prussian prisoners of war, while non-commissioned soldiers were to be selected from Prussians who had deserted the Prussian Army. The Regiment was formed as an infantry regiment with three battalions. Most notable taking apart of Napoleons Iberia Campaign where the Dos de Mayo Uprising would be their first real deployment, 4e is one of the only regiments in French service who would use a Black coats, and Red Facings

Upon enlisting, Conscrits will be placed in the Jeune Garde Company. In this company they will be trained and given knowledge of artillery crewing and combat. The company itself often participate in 1v1s, ensuring that they're ready for combat and to build their experience in artillery.

Once they reach the rank of Cannonier 1er, they're eligible for draft to our Cheval unit. Cheval officers will scout 7eme rankers that are ready to be part of the elites. Rankers must show great activity, attitude and performance in all artillery events.
